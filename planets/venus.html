Venus: Earth's Fiery Sister

What is it?

Venus is the brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon, captivating observers with its brilliant shine. Yet beneath its luminous appearance lies a world of extreme conditions, making it one of the most inhospitable places in the solar system.

Characteristics of Venus

Thick Atmosphere and Runaway Greenhouse Effect: Venus boasts a dense atmosphere composed primarily of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid, making it the poster child for the greenhouse effect gone awry. This thick atmosphere traps heat, raising surface temperatures to around 465 degrees Celsius (869 degrees Fahrenheit)—hot enough to melt lead and hotter than the surface of Mercury, despite being farther from the Sun.

Surface Conditions: The surface of Venus is rocky, with thousands of volcanoes—more than any other planet in the solar system. It features vast plains covered in volcanic flows, mountain ranges, and several large impact craters. Interestingly, due to the high surface temperatures and atmospheric pressure, no liquid water exists on the surface, and the rocks may even glow slightly from their own heat.

Slow Rotation and Retrograde Motion: Venus rotates on its axis very slowly and in the opposite direction to most planets in the solar system. A day on Venus (one full rotation on its axis) lasts longer than a Venusian year (one orbit around the Sun). This peculiar rotation pattern results in the Sun rising in the west and setting in the east on Venus.

Interesting Facts about Venus

Volcanic Activity: Venus is covered in volcanic landscapes, with evidence suggesting that some of these volcanoes are still active today, which would make Venus one of the only volcanically active planets in the solar system besides Earth.

Extreme Atmospheric Pressure: Extreme Atmospheric Pressure: The atmospheric pressure on Venus is crushing—about 92 times that of Earth’s, equivalent to the pressure at a depth of about 900 meters (3,000 feet) underwater on Earth.

Sulfuric Acid Rain: High in the clouds of Venus, sulfuric acid forms raindrops that evaporate before reaching the surface due to the high temperatures, a phenomenon known as virga.

Technical Facts about Venus

Diameter: Approximately 12,104 kilometers (7,521 miles), making it very close in size to Earth.

Orbit: Venus orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 108 million kilometers (67 million miles) and takes about 225 Earth days to complete one orbit.

Rotation: The planet rotates on its axis every 243 Earth days, but because it rotates in the opposite direction to its orbit, a day from sunrise to sunrise (a solar day) lasts 117 Earth days.

Gravity: About 90% of Earth’s, which means a 100 kg person would weigh about 90 kg on Venus.

Atmosphere: Composed mostly of carbon dioxide, with clouds containing sulfuric acid, making any potential for life as we know it impossible under current conditions.
